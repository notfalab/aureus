2. Specific Risks of Forex Trading

Market Risk

Gold prices can be highly volatile and influenced by numerous factors including economic data, geopolitical events, central bank policies, and market sentiment. Prices can move rapidly against your position.

Leverage Risk

Forex trading typically involves leverage, which amplifies both potential profits and losses. A small market movement can result in proportionally larger gains or losses. You may lose more than your initial deposit.

Liquidity Risk

During periods of high volatility or low liquidity, it may be difficult to execute trades at desired prices. Slippage can occur, resulting in worse execution prices than expected.

Gap Risk

Prices can "gap" significantly between trading sessions, particularly over weekends or during major news events. Stop-loss orders may not protect you from gap moves.

3. Risks Specific to Automated Trading

  • Technical Failures: Software bugs, connectivity issues, or server problems can prevent trades from executing properly or cause unintended trades.
  • Strategy Limitations: No trading strategy works in all market conditions. Strategies that performed well historically may fail in changing markets.
  • Over-Optimization: Past performance shown in backtests may not be indicative of future results. Historical results can be overfitted to past data.
  • Execution Delays: Network latency or broker issues can cause delays in trade execution, affecting results.
  • System Requirements: The bot requires a stable internet connection and properly configured MetaTrader 5 to function correctly.
